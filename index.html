Body Mass Index Tracker

Body mass index (BMI) is a value derived from the mass (weight) and height of a person. The BMI is defined as the body mass divided by the square of the body height. A BMI between 18.5 and 25 kg/m² indicates a normal weight. A BMI of less than 18.5 kg/m² is considered underweight. A BMI between 25 kg/m² and 29.9 kg/m² is considered overweight. A BMI of 30 kg/m² or higher is considered obese.

The purpose of this application is to help monitor an individuals BMI and track their progress.

If BMI is under 18.5 kg/m², what should I do?

Begin by exercising regularly and slightly increasing your calorie intake. According to the Academy of Nutrition and Dietetics, increasing your calories by at least 200 per day can help you gain lean muscle mass in a healthy way.

If BMI is above 18.5 kg/m² and 25 kg/m², what should I do?

In theory you are healthy and you are neither obesse or malnourished, but as stated above exercise with a healthy caloric intake is needed to stay within these BMI ranges.

If BMI is above 25.0 kg/m², what should I do?

A high BMI theoretically means you are obesse and need to drop your BMI levels. Exercising regularly with a managed healthy diet and tracking claorie intake will lead to promising results.
